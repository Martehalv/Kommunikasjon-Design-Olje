Beneath the Surface:
Understanding Norway’s Offshore Oil Work

How Brede and his crew kept the wells alive far out at sea.

One of those workers is Brede, who spent seven years on board the vessel Island Wellserver, a specialized ship built for servicing and repairing oil wells on the seabed. While on board between 2011 and 2018, Brede helped ensure that the flow of oil and gas remained steady and safe.

“I started as an RLWI operator, which is working directly with the equipment used on the seabed. And in 2015, I became the shift leader on board,” he says.

RLWI stands for Riserless Light Well Intervention.
